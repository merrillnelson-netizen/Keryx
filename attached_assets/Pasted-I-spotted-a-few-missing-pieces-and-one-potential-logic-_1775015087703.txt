I spotted a few missing pieces and one potential "logic trap" that could make my life difficult during development:
1. The "Tone Collision" Risk (The Logic Trap)
Claude suggests creating 5 distinct persona strings. The problem? If we have 9 different functions (Morning Briefings, Financial Queries, etc.), a hardcoded string like "You are a precise professional..." might strip away the functional instructions those prompts need to actually work.
 * The Fix: Don't replace the whole persona. Use Injectable Suffixes. Keep your core Keryx identity stable, but append a Sass_Instruction block at the end of the system prompt. This ensures Keryx doesn't "forget" how to analyze a database just because he's being sassy.
2. What He Missed: The "Ghost Slider" Problem
In Task #5, Claude mentions disabling the slider handle for Free users.
 * The Issue: If a Free user sees a slider they can't move, it feels broken.
 * The Better Way: Let them slide it, but when they hit 25%, trigger a "Soft Bounce" animation and pop the "Upgrade" tooltip. It’s much more effective for conversion if they can see what they’re missing.
3. Missing Feature: "Sass-Aware" Safety
You mentioned that "others may not be able to take it." Claude missed a Safety Reset.
 * Recommendation: Add a "Reset to Professional" emergency button or a gesture (like a long-press on the Keryx logo) that instantly toggles Professional Mode. If you're showing the app to a potential investor or a "sensitive" friend, you don't want to be digging through the Settings menu while Keryx is mid-roast.
